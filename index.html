The Importance of Monitoring

Diabetic Foot Ulcers (DFUs) are a leading cause of lower-extremity amputation worldwide. Early detection of pressure hotspots and subtle skin changes can dramatically reduce complications.

Device & System Design

The FootGuard device integrates pressure sensors, wireless communication, and an AI-based risk assessment engine into a single, clinician-friendly platform.

Smart Insole Architecture

  • High-density plantar pressure sensor array
  • Flexible, low-profile form factor for daily wear
  • Temperature and moisture sensing (optional)

Connectivity & Data Flow

  • BLE connection to patient mobile app
  • Secure cloud sync to clinician dashboard
  • Real-time alerting for threshold breaches

AI Risk Stratification

The AI model combines pressure patterns, wearing time, and historical ulcer events to classify patients into low, medium, and high-risk cohorts, enabling proactive outreach.

Symptoms of DFUs

  • Persistent redness or localized warmth in the foot
  • Swelling around bony prominences or pressure points
  • Open sores or wounds that are slow to heal
  • Pain, tingling, burning, or numbness
  • Discoloration, callus buildup, or skin breakdown

Early Warning Focus

FootGuard surfaces subtle changes in pressure distribution and activity patterns before visible symptoms appear, giving clinicians a window to intervene early.

Types of Treatment

1. Wound Care & Dressings

Regular debridement, moisture-balanced dressings, and infection control protocols to support healing.

2. Offloading & Footwear

Custom insoles, boots, and footwear designed to redistribute pressure away from vulnerable areas.

3. Antibiotic Therapy

Targeted treatment for soft-tissue and bone infections, guided by cultures and imaging.

4. Surgical Intervention

In advanced cases, surgical offloading, debridement, or reconstruction may be required to restore function and prevent amputation.

Clinical Advice

Routine Foot Examinations

Perform structured foot exams at every visit: inspect skin, nails, and pressure zones; assess pulses and sensation; document findings clearly.

Follow-Up Workflow

  • Review FootGuard alerts and high-risk patients daily.
  • Prioritize outreach to newly elevated-risk individuals.
  • Coordinate care with podiatry, wound care, and vascular teams.
